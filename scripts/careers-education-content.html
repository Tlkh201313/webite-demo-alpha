At Northstowe Learning Community, we believe every student should have access to real-world, relevant learning. Careers information is critical when making decisions about next steps and future choices. Our CEIAG programme is flexible and adapts as new opportunities arise.

We have a dedicated team of knowledgeable professionals who help, support and teach students about the choices available. Our aim is to make sure that every student achieves their full potential.

Education and training after 16

All students must continue in education or training until the end of the academic year in which they turn 18. This does not mean they must stay in school. Options include:

  • Full-time education (for example at a school or college)
  • An apprenticeship
  • Part-time education or training combined with employment, self-employment or volunteering for 20 hours or more per week

More information on choices and how parents can help is on the Meridian Pathways website.

Measuring impact

The careers programme is monitored annually. Evaluation includes:

  • A review of destination data as an outcome of the careers programme
  • A review of the programme against the Gatsby Benchmarks
  • A review of data from Unifrog that feeds into the Gatsby Benchmarks
  • A review of the Future Skills Questionnaire completed in Year 7 and Year 10

We have achieved the national Quality in Careers Standard using the Career Mark approach from Complete Careers — official recognition of high-quality careers provision for all students at Northstowe Learning Community.
