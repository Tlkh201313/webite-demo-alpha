At Northstowe Secondary College, we work together to keep a pleasant, purposeful atmosphere where every student lives our values: kind, curious and hardworking.

We want every student to feel part of a calm, respectful college where high standards are the norm — not the exception.

We equip students with the values, attitudes, skills and knowledge for success in future employment, study and life. Academic achievement matters — and educating the whole person matters just as much.

What we expect

Students should show through their actions that they understand appropriate behaviour, know what is expected of them, and respond accordingly. That is reflected in our college and Meridian Trust values:

  • favorite Kind
  • psychology Curious
  • school Hardworking

emoji_events Recognition

We believe in recognising all students. Every child is different, and it can be hard to acknowledge everyone in a large school. Our aim is for every student to feel valued and appreciated for their effort to live the school’s values and try their very best.

Recognition celebrates effort, progress and achievement — and motivates others. It strengthens relationships between staff and students. Opportunities are given in lessons or shared with home as celebratory comments.

  • record_voice_over Praise by name in lessons and around college
  • call Positive calls and emails home
  • mail Subject and Northstowe postcards
  • newspaper Newsletter and assembly celebrations
  • share Facebook and end-of-term awards
  • poll Termly student and twice-yearly parent surveys

Our aim is to capture something positive and wonderful for every child, every year. We reflect on our processes if surveys show we are not meeting our own high standards.

gavel Sanctions

Staff work in partnership with families so students develop high levels of self-discipline and a proper regard for authority, taking responsibility for their actions appropriate to their age and maturity.

We seek a kind, curious and hardworking learning environment by:

  • check_circle Demonstrating good behaviour and discipline
  • check_circle Promoting excellent learning, teaching and attainment
  • check_circle Responding fairly, consistently and proportionately
  • check_circle Promoting positive relationships across the community
  • check_circle Ensuring early intervention
  • check_circle Providing a safe environment free from disruption and abuse

We resolve issues restoratively to protect healthy relationships and ensure those who cause harm accept responsibility for the impact of their actions.

Below is a one-page summary of our behaviour policy. The full policy is on our Policies and Statutory Information page.

groups Be Northstowe

Most students contribute positively and meet expectations consistently. When reminders are needed, students follow Be Northstowe in and around college.

If expectations are not met in or out of lessons, there are clear consequences. The same standards apply when travelling to and from college — misbehaviour is not ignored.

Consequences are applied consistently and fairly. Tutors work with students and families to prevent recurrence. More serious incidents, including bullying, are handled by House Links or SLT with sanctions at an appropriate level.
