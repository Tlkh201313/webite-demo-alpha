Students

Well done for getting this far. It is normal to go through struggles. Often the best first step is to talk to a friend, family member, teacher or doctor. The sites below can help you understand what you are going through and how to get extra support.

  • Anna Freud — On My Mind — 85 self-care ideas to plan into your week. Pick what you enjoy; not every tip will suit you.

  • YoungMinds — Conditions, feelings and symptoms, plus strategies. Try “Find help” then “Feelings and symptoms” or “Conditions”.

  • Keep Your Head — Cambridgeshire information for young people, with links to specialist support.

  • ChatHealth — Confidential text service with healthcare professionals. Text 07480 635443. You should get a reply within a day.

  • Mind — National charity with an A–Z of mental health and real stories from people who have found ways to cope.

  • Kooth — Online chat with professional counsellors about how you feel, even if you are not sure what is wrong.

  • Centre 33 — Local charity offering advice and support for young people in Cambridgeshire up to age 25.

  • Samaritans — Available 24/7 for anyone in distress. Call 116 123 (free).

  • Childline — Private, confidential support for children and young people up to 19. No problem is too big or small.

  • The Kite Trust — One-to-one support for LGBT+ young people under 25 in Cambridgeshire, including youth groups (including in Ely).

    They also run online hangouts and a newsletter with links, craft and recipe ideas. Sign up on their website.

  • stem4 — Charity supporting positive teenage mental health, with apps and education resources.

  • The Mix — Support on money, housing, jobs, relationships, drugs and more — online, social or free helpline.

Parents and carers

  • Minded for families — Tutorials on what your child may be experiencing, with symptoms, strategies and professional links.

  • Mind — A–Z of mental health language plus stories from people who have learnt to manage difficulties.

  • YoungMinds — parents — Helps parents understand what their child may be going through and how to help.

Mental health strategy

At Northstowe Secondary College we care about the mental health and wellbeing of all students. See our strategy below. Our Feeling worried or anxious? page has more support services and self-help resources.

Intent

We believe mental health and wellbeing is everybody's business — governors, students, parents, teaching, admin and support staff all have a role in promoting it.

Implementation

We are working towards the Carnegie Mental Health Award in Schools, combining a whole-school positive culture with targeted actions.

We aim for an environment where students are resilient, eager to learn, feel valued and look forward to school. Staff model good practice in their own wellbeing.

Key procedures:

  • Simple, clear mental health and wellbeing strategy.
  • Mental health and wellbeing lead working with a trained Mental Health Champion (Place2Be).
  • Raise awareness through assemblies, How to Thrive curriculum, staff training, parent sessions, newsletters and displays.
  • Weekly How to Thrive (1 hour) teaching resilience and five coping strategies:
    • Relaxation activities (linked to Sport England physical activity plan)
    • Thinking about something good
    • Leaving a situation
    • Talking to someone
    • Mental games
  • Support through the Mental Health and Wellbeing Wave / The Nest (e.g. YMCA counselling, dog and equine therapy) and partnership with other agencies.
  • Student-led wellbeing initiatives (e.g. creation of Space Place).
  • Staff wellbeing programme through structured CPD.
  • Securing the Carnegie Mental Health in Schools Award.

We are delighted to confirm that Northstowe Secondary College has been awarded the Carnegie Centre of Excellence School Mental Health Award — Silver Status. Award lead Mr Russell continues this work passionately with students.
